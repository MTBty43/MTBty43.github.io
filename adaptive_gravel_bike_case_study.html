Designing an ultralight adaptive bike frame for a rider who needed one to fit exactly right.

A component design project built in partnership with the Crested Butte Adaptive Sports Center — balancing strength, weight, and manufacturability using SLS titanium and bonded carbon fiber.

01

The Problem

Off-the-shelf adaptive bike frames rarely fit the rider they're built for — geometry, load paths, and adjustability all need to be custom. The Crested Butte Adaptive Sports Center needed a frame that could be built lighter than existing options without compromising the structural margins a rider with different load patterns depends on.

02

Design Approach

The frame needed to hit a strength-to-weight target that standard steel or aluminum construction couldn't reach at this geometry, so material selection became the central tradeoff of the project.

MATERIAL SLS-printed titanium front triangle + bonded carbon fiber rear stays — titanium where load paths were complex and hard to predict, carbon fiber where loads were more axial and predictable.
METHOD Applied failure mechanics principles to rider-specific load cases rather than generic industry load standards, since the rider's weight distribution and riding style didn't match a standard-build rider.
CONSTRAINT Every design decision was checked against manufacturability — SLS titanium print orientation, and controlling bond-line quality for the carbon fiber joints.

Outcome
