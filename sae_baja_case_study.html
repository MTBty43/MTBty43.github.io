A senior capstone project building a custom off-road vehicle to SAE Baja competition rules — under real cost, time, and reliability constraints, with a team depending on the schedule holding.

01

The Problem

SAE Baja vehicles are built to a strict rulebook and judged on both performance and cost — every subsystem (suspension, drivetrain, chassis, steering) has to work together under a fixed budget and a hard competition deadline, with no room for a subsystem to blow the schedule for the rest of the team.

02

Approach

As systems engineer, the job was less about any single part and more about making sure every subsystem's decisions stayed compatible with everyone else's — geometry, mounting points, weight budget, and timeline all had to reconcile across the team.

CAD Owned the master assembly model so every subsystem team could check interference and mounting geometry against a single source of truth instead of guessing at fit.
PLANNING Built and maintained the manufacturing plan and build schedule, sequencing fabrication so long-lead parts didn't stall final assembly close to competition.
CONSTRAINT Every subsystem decision was weighed against the competition's cost report — cheaper, simpler solutions were preferred over more capable but expensive ones.
